How It Works

This AI opponent was trained using a Genetic Algorithm to evolve neural networks that play Pong.

Training Process:

  • Neural Network Architecture: A feedforward network with 10 input features (paddle/ball positions, velocities, time-to-intercept predictions) and 1 continuous output (paddle velocity in [-1, 1]).
  • Genetic Algorithm: A population of 50 neural networks evolves over generations through selection, crossover, and mutation.
  • Fitness Function: Networks are evaluated based on scoring points, hitting the ball, returning shots, and penalties for missing, camping, or being idle.
  • Curriculum Learning: Early generations play against a rule-based teacher, then progress to playing against the current population, and finally against a Hall of Fame of best historical models.
  • State Mirroring: The AI uses a symmetric state representation, so it performs equally well on both left and right sides without retraining.

Technical Details:

  • Framework: PyTorch for neural networks, Pygame for game simulation
  • Training: ~300 generations with population size 50, resulting in a model that can reliably return shots and play competitively
  • State Features: Normalized paddle/ball positions, velocities, signed distances, time-to-intercept, and predicted intercept point
  • Action Space: Continuous control (float in [-1, 1]) instead of discrete actions, allowing smoother gameplay

The model you're playing against was trained for hundreds of generations, learning through trial and error which strategies lead to higher fitness scores (winning games, hitting the ball, avoiding penalties).
